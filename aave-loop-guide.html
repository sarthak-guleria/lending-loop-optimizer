A Practical Guide to Yield Looping on Aave on Mantle

How to put idle capital to work using sUSDe, GHO, and Aave E-Mode — step by step. Written for people who know crypto but haven't touched DeFi lending yet.

This is not financial advice. DeFi carries real risks including liquidation, smart contract bugs, and yield compression. Understand what you're doing before deploying capital.

With markets moving sideways, people, founders, and treasuries are waking up to something they overlooked when prices were flying: idle capital has an opportunity cost.

When you're not in a trade, your money is still making a decision. It's just usually a bad one — sitting in a wallet earning nothing while DeFi protocols pay 5-8% on stablecoins. The infrastructure has matured. The yields are real. And right now, Aave on Mantle is running an incentive program that makes it a particularly good time to explore this.

I'll walk you through a loop strategy you can adjust based on your own risk appetite and capital size. No trading, no perpetuals, no complex mechanics. Just lending, borrowing, and putting that borrowed capital back to work.

Key Concepts Before We Start

Supplying and Borrowing

Aave is a lending protocol. You deposit assets (supply), which earns you interest. You can then borrow against your deposited assets, paying a borrow rate. The ratio of what you've borrowed to what you've deposited is your Loan-to-Value (LTV).

Health Factor

Your health factor is the single number that tells you how safe your position is:

Health Factor = (Collateral Value × Liquidation Threshold) / Total Debt

If it drops below 1.0, your position gets liquidated. Above 1.5 is comfortable. Below 1.3 starts getting risky.

E-Mode (Efficiency Mode)

Aave's E-Mode allows correlated assets to use a much higher LTV than normal. On Mantle, the sUSDe Stablecoins E-Mode lets you supply sUSDe as collateral and borrow USDC/GHO at up to 90% LTV with a 92% liquidation threshold. Both assets are dollar-pegged, so the protocol can afford the higher LTV.

sUSDe

sUSDe is Ethena's yield-bearing stablecoin. It appreciates as Ethena earns funding rates from delta-neutral perpetual futures positions. Historically it's earned between 4.7% and 15%+ APY. Currently around 3.5%. Dollar-pegged, not algorithmic — the yield comes from real derivatives activity.

GHO — and why it's the benchmark

GHO is Aave's native stablecoin. On Mantle, Aave governance is currently incentivising GHO supply at ~7% APY. That's our benchmark — what you'd earn doing the simplest thing. The honest take: GHO's 7% is a governance subsidy that can be cut overnight with no warning. sUSDe's yield is structural — compresses gradually, has a floor, and you can see it coming. The loop builds on structural yield and captures the GHO incentive as a bonus while it lasts.

The Core Loop Mechanic

Supply sUSDe as collateral, borrow USDC against it, convert that USDC back to sUSDe, and supply again. Each loop increases your total sUSDe exposure relative to your starting capital.

On the final loop, instead of converting back to sUSDe, you borrow GHO directly on Aave and supply it — capturing the 7% incentive rate. GHO is borrowable in E-Mode directly on Mantle. No bridging required, stays on chain.

Strategy overview — 5-step loop mechanics with key risks

Every loop drops your health factor. More loops = more yield but less protection. The strategy below is deliberately conservative — the marginal APY from each extra loop shrinks while the risk grows.

Why We're Keeping Loops Low

You could loop 7-8 times and push APY higher. Here's why we don't:

  • At 7-8 loops your health factor is ~1.2. A 3% sUSDe depeg — which has happened historically — puts you in the danger zone.
  • Borrow rates are variable. USDC borrow on Aave on Mantle can creep from 2% to 2.5%+ as utilisation increases. At high loop counts this compresses your spread meaningfully.
  • Unwinding is sequential — exiting 7 loops means 7 sets of repay → withdraw → swap. At 2 loops it's manageable. At 7 it's a chore under pressure.
  • Diminishing returns. Loop 1 to 2 adds ~0.2% APY. Loop 6 to 7 adds ~0.06%. The risk-reward flips fast.

Target health factor: 1.5 minimum, 1.7 comfortable.

Step by Step

What you need

  • A wallet with a small amount of MNT on Mantle for gas (cents per transaction)
  • sUSDe — mint on Ethena's app or swap into it on Mantle
  • Matcha or Bungee for DEX aggregation when swapping USDC back to sUSDe between loops
  • Aave V3 — go to app.aave.com and switch network to Mantle

Building the position

  1. Get sUSDe and go to Aave on Mantle. Enable E-Mode: click 'E-Mode' in the borrows panel, select 'sUSDe Stablecoins'.
  2. Supply your sUSDe as collateral. Toggle it on as collateral.
  3. Borrow USDC at 80% of your collateral value — 90% E-Mode max minus 10% safety buffer.
  4. Go to Matcha or Bungee. Swap the borrowed USDC back to sUSDe. Return to Aave and supply it. Loop 1 complete.
  5. Repeat steps 3–4 once more. Loop 2 complete. Health factor should be around 1.56.
  6. Final step: use remaining borrow capacity to borrow GHO directly on Aave. Supply it immediately. You're now earning 7% on that GHO within the same protocol.
  7. You're in. Monitor health factor. If it drops below 1.4, repay some USDC debt or supply more sUSDe.

Personal rule: if health factor drops below 1.4, act. Repay some USDC debt or supply more sUSDe. Don't wait until 1.1.

Risks to Understand Before You Start

sUSDe APY compression

If Ethena's funding rates drop to 1-2%, your spread against borrowing costs shrinks and the loop may underperform GHO direct supply. This doesn't cause liquidation — it just means you'd have been better off doing something simpler.

Borrow rate creep

USDC borrow rates on Aave are utilisation-driven. Modelled at 2.5% as a stressed rate — if it goes above 3%, revisit your loop depth.

GHO incentive ending

The 7% GHO supply APY is a governance program. When it ends, the GHO you supplied drops to base rates (~2-3%). Manageable — you redeploy that capital — but worth planning for.

Smart contract risk

Aave is one of the most battle-tested protocols in DeFi. Ethena is newer but well-audited and growing fast. Single chain on Mantle means no bridge exposure.

How to Exit

Exiting is the reverse of entering, one loop at a time: repay GHO → withdraw sUSDe → swap to USDC via Matcha or Bungee → repay USDC. Repeat per loop. At loop 2 this is ~6 transactions. Always repay before withdrawing.
